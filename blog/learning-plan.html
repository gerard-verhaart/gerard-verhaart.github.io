Bootcamp Learning Plan

09 February 2023

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

To use my experience from this course to become an experienced developer, and really grow into a role I can get behind and dedicate myself to.

My plan is to return to my old company but as a Junior Developer and really get into and take ownership of a particular software product. My impact would be to eventually become the resident expert on this particular product and become a company leader in this space.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I have a strength that I've been in the workplace for a good decade, and I know a lot about different types of IT and companies and can mentor other students on what to expect in the real world.

My biggest limitation is that I haven't studied for a while, so it's a real change of pace to put my ego aside and allow myself to be mentored.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

The fact that I've come from a professional background and I'm upskilling myself in a new field means that I have a lot of real-world experience for others to draw upon, and I am confident in my own abilities. My biggest non-technical challenge will be to put my own learning first (as much as I want to help others) and focus on being a good student and stretching my technical and human skills as much as possible.

I love to take control of projects, so one of my focuses will be on sharing the workload - making sure I'm not trying to do everything myself, and focus on working within a team instead of being the team.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

The main ones are leadership and interpersonal skills - being able to work with others, facilitate teamwork, and sharing the workload. I need to work on my listening and patience, and really make sure that I work well with my teammates.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Same as Foundations in a way - be honest with me and tell me if I'm starting to stray off the path you need me to be on. Also, be there if I really need help, and really support our team to work well together.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

To be punctual, study hard, push myself to do extra challenges as I can in order to maximise my learning from this course, and to work well within teams and groups, as well as partner work.

I expect myself to keep an open mind especially on the non-technical skill portions, and to approach every part with good faith and determination. Also, to be mindful of other peoples different backgrounds and personalities and be open-minded.


Foundations Learning Plan

19 January 2023

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

Tricky question - it's hard to see the path until you're walking the path. In the immediate future my goal is to use this Bootcamp to get me a role as a Junior Programmer / Developer with my employer, Fingermark. The future depends on how I use this career change from a Support role to become more experienced and learn new technologies. Based on my experience with a bit of luck and hard work, this could lead me to a Team Lead / Senior Developer / Management role.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I'm a fast learner - it doesn't take me too long to aquire and start using new concepts, and I'm also a skilled communicator. My weaknesses is that I like things to be right, and done my way. As such, I make sure that everything is roughly done and then go back and focus on the bits I feel still need to be completed, and I also take extra care on reading the questions in case my interpretation is incorrect.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to work on my team skills - working as a team, developing together, mentor/learning roles. In real life, developers normally work as teams that split out their bits of work and then pull it all together at the end of each sprint - as such, improving teamwork and soft skills is essential for any developer.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I have already made sure that over the next few months when this course runs, I will not have distractions or commitments that detract from my learning. I am also aware that I have a wealth of real-world experience in the IT industry - however, this means I need to be careful of how much I share and always put my learning as the top priority. It also means I may have learned some bad habits that need to be "unlearned" in order for me to be the best developer and person I can be. Working productively and safely with others - I will focus on integrity and productivity, making sure that I am fair and focused. If things build up I will be seeking assistance with facilitators and cutting out any distractions and non-essential things, as well as managing my time and my breaks to bring things back to a steady level. I will make sure that my time with industry/community representatives is saved/delayed until the Careers week(s), so that we are working on this together after the course content is covered.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I have already struck this with this week (Sprint 2)'s work - there have been some blockers that I needed to reach out for help with. As such, I am now more comfortable with how long I should work on a problem and researching various results on the internet and experimenting before I reach out for help from the facilitators. As a rule of thumb, I have figured out that the optimum time to reach out is either half an hour or whenever trying to fix the issue is no longer "fun" - whichever comes first.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I want to be told if I'm heading off the path, or if my expectations or directions are starting to go in the wrong direction. I value honesty above all else, and this is critical to my learning journey. In other words, be brutally honest with me and let's head off any potential issues before they become problems.
