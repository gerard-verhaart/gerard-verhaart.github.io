Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticitys main foundation is that IQ is not a fixed variable, and can be trained and increased by absolutely anyone. It also dives into the concept that putting more effort into learning and practice can not only teach you a subject, but also make you smarter by exercising your neural pathways in the brain.

Part of the study goes into how practicing a task mentally can give you almost the exact same benefit as if you do it for real - even physical exercise. As a result, it opens you up to improving on things even if you can't physically do them where you are. As a result, time spent on a bus or a plane where you are a passenger can be used for mental practice.

Anything that engages your brain is technically an exercise in improving neuroplasticity. Video and Board Games? Check. Learn a language? Check. Travel and Exercise? Check and Check. Talk to friends? Check. As a result, the best way to increase neuroplasticity is to look at your daily routine and build in tasks and events that help you build cognitive abilities. I have a board game group I go to once a week for example, and it's fairly large and social. Learning new games and engaging with new people helps to build my neuroplasticity.

Growth Mindset

Having a Growth Mindset means knowing that with practice we will get better at something, especially if we attempt difficult or challenging tasks. Growth Mindset is about believing in yourself and knowing that your current abilities do not define you - that putting effort into new things will help us to improve. It links to Neuroplasticity in that having the right mindset and constant practice of challenging tasks can improve our intelligence and skills.

I've heard of these concepts before, however the studies where they put this into place around schools and learning environments make perfect sense - having the right attitude while learning makes a huge difference into the effort you put in and the rewards you get out.

Growth Mindset is a frame of mind - the best way to integrate it with my learning journey is to practice it with the meditation segment of each day - being positive and open to new experiences, and attempting tasks even when they are outside my skillset knowing that practice makes anything achievable.
